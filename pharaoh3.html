Ancient Egypt - Pharaoh Tutankhamun Details

Pharaoh Tutankhamun - The Boy King

Pharaoh Tutankhamun, also known as King Tut, is one of the most famous pharaohs of ancient Egypt. He became pharaoh at a young age and ruled during the 18th Dynasty of the New Kingdom period.

Tutankhamun is known for his tomb, which was discovered nearly intact in the Valley of the Kings. The treasures found within the tomb provide valuable insights into the art, culture, and beliefs of ancient Egypt.

Despite his short reign, Tutankhamun made significant contributions to religious restoration and artistic revival. He reversed the religious reforms of his predecessor and reinstated the worship of traditional Egyptian gods.

Exploring the life of Tutankhamun allows us to glimpse into the world of ancient Egypt and the complexities of pharaonic rule during the New Kingdom.
